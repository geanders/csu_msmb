Bernoulli distribution A probability distribution to describe the outcome probability for two possible events.
parameter A numerical value that describes a population or a model.
complementary A description of two events who are mutually exclusive and whose probabilities sum to one. In other words, either one event or the other is guaranteed to happen, but not both.
binomial random variable A variable whose values occur according to a binomial probability distribution.

Poisson distribution A probability distribution that is appropriate when the probability of success is small and the number of trials is large.
epitope Site on a macromolecular antigen to which an antibody binds. This is the part of an antigen that is recognized by the immune system and is also known as an antigen determinent.
Enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA) An assay that is used to detect specific epitopes at different positions along a protein.

Monte Carlo method A method that uses a computer simulation based on our generative model that finds the probabilities of the events we’re interested in.
